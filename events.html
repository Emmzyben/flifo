Intouch magazine featured article : Breaking traditional norms around STEM education

Breaking biases, and ethnic and traditional norms around girls in stem education, promoting men's involvement, women's advocating for other women, modest resources, and women's empowerment through stem careers is crucial to promote gender equality and providing equal opportunities for girls and women in STEM fields. There are several ways to achieve this, including promoting men's involvement, women's advocacy, and women's empowerment through STEM careers. Firstly, promoting men's involvement in STEM education and careers can help break gender stereotypes and biases. Men can be advocates for women in STEM and help to create a more inclusive and supportive environment. Men can encourage girls to pursue STEM education and careers and serve as role models for young women interested in these fields. Recently, I discussed the benefit of gaining the support of men as collaborators with the Founder / Executive Director of Flourishing Life International Foundation in Nigeria, West Africa, in my video podcast, InTouch Think STEAM Career Podcast. Concept for the She4STEM initiative in schools and communities

STEM IS THE FUTURE OF WORK About The SHE4STEM project The She is for STEM ( SHE4STEM) is an initiative of Flourishing Life International Foundation created to promote the advancement of STEM education and careers in schools in Nigeria by identifying, inspiring, including and building the innovative human capacities of girls towards the development and deployment of practical applications of science, technology, engineering and mathematics. STEM is the Future!!! Vision: To develop and drive initiatives aimed at closing the STEM talent gaps in Nigeria; bringing girls and women to the forefront of STEM education and careers Mission To engage in identification and development of targeted policies geared towards breaking of existing socio- cultural barriers around STEM; with a concomitant deployment of advanced learning technologies aimed at raising the next generation of STEM work force and making STEM education and careers inclusive and attractive for girls and women in Africa. Executive summary The main goal of the project is to provide young school girls in underserved communities in FCT and our STEM / STEAM clubs in our state branches in Nigeria with access to Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to develop their digital literacy and technological skills....

Projects' Report 2018 - 2021

Introduction: In a bid to bridge the gap, FLIFO organized a one day Medical Mission on Saturday, March 10, 2018 which was held outside the Chief Palace, Waru Community in Abuja Municipal Area Council (AMAC), FCT. The outreach was the foundation’s inaugural activity and an effort to commemorate the International Women’s Day (IWD –annual event) which was celebrated globally on March 8, 2018 with the theme - TIME IS NOW: RURAL AND URBAN ACTIVISTS TRANSFORMING WOMEN’S LIVES. The exercise was aimed at : 1. Increasing access to preventive health services for rural populations. 2. Providing cultural appropriate health education 3. Providing access to basic preventive health services- Testing and Screening and referral to a close by medical facility for follow up 4. Providing Counselling and Support services on terminal and infectious diseases 5. Improving referral and linkages 6. Capacity development for women on need to stand out to be bold for a change Evidence based outcomes: 1. A registration stand for the following • Demographical data intake. • Blood pressure measurement • Body weight and height measurement • Calculation of BMI and interpretation for the participants

2022 Project Report: International Women's Day

Theme: Sustaining the 21st Century woman: cushioning the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. #BreakTheBias. Date: 26th - 27th March 2022 Project Objectives: An Initiative to harness the power of creativity to inspire positive change and shift behavior for the SDGs. This involves collective and coordinated actions that mobilize people in communities, traditional rulers, community stakeholders, women leaders, parents and women from different sectors in women capacity development to flip the Script and achieve the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development Outreach itinerary: As a pre - event activity, valued collaboration was made with the women network of the Flourishing Life international named Ladies in Sustainable Community Development International coordinated by Aunty Willy Willy – a popular on - air personality on the most popular radio station in Abuja (WAZOBIA FM). We had a radio presentation to address the socio-cultural norms and biases affecting the inclusion of women /girls into STEM education and careers. Also we answered the questions from the public on full meaning of STEM, the state of STEM in Nigeria, the collective roles of parents and the community in breaking the age long biases. We also enlightened the public on the greatest challenges, the benefits of STEM careers in national development and meaningful careers for women and girls, the solutions we are working as an NGO and collaborative opportunities towards breaking the biases and including more girls/women to pursue STEM education and careers.
